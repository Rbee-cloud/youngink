Latest Submissions

Paper Planes

by Liam K.
I folded my dreams into paper planes, Creased with hope and little pains. Threw them out the window high, To watch them dance against the sky. Some crashed down in the muddy rain, Dissolving ink, dissolving pain. But one caught wind and flew so far, Past the moon and to a star. It carries words I dare not speak, To finds the future that I seek.

The Old Library

by Chloe W.
The dust motes danced in the shafts of afternoon light that pierced the gloom of the stacks. It smelled of vanilla and decaying paper—the best smell in the world. I ran my fingers along the spines, feeling the leather and cloth, the embossed titles of stories waiting to be woken up. I wasn't looking for anything specific, just an escape. My exams were looming, my parents were arguing, and the world outside felt too loud and too fast. But in here, time moved differently. It moved at the pace of a turning page. I pulled a blue volume from the shelf, 'The Starless Sea', and sat on the floor. For the next three hours, I wasn't Chloe the student. I was a traveler in a world beneath the surface of the earth.

Coffee Shop sketches

by Alex M.
She sits in the corner, charcoal in hand. The barista shouts names—coffee orders, demand. She draws the steam rising, the clink of a spoon, A snapshot of life in the dull afternoon. Nobody notices the art being made, How moments are captured before they can fade.

About YoungInk

YoungInk is a digital sanctuary for the creative minds of tomorrow. We believe that young adulthood is a time of intense emotion, profound realization, and rapid growth. Our mission is to provide a minimalist, distraction-free canvas where the words and art of young creators can shine without the noise of social media. We value authenticity over virality.
